about me

I read data for what it's actually saying, not what's convenient.

I'm a Computer Science & Engineering graduate from BRAC University (CGPA 3.83/4.00) working as a data analyst. Day to day, that means cleaning messy datasets, building dashboards in Power BI, and running the kind of predictive models that need to hold up when someone challenges the number — not just look good in a slide.

That instinct came from my thesis: a two-stage deep learning pipeline for depression detection, where every result had to clear a statistical significance test before it counted. I bring the same discipline to business analytics — correlation isn't the finish line, and a dashboard is only useful if the number behind it is defensible.

Outside of work, I've captained football teams for eight-plus years and competed in regional Math Olympiads — both taught me the same thing in different clothes: stay analytical under pressure, and a result only counts if the whole team gets there together.
